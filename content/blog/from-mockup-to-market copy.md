Over the last few years, the Pokémon Trading Card Game has changed dramatically. Sets are larger, chase cards are rarer, and alternate art cards have become some of the most sought-after collectibles in the hobby.

For collectors and players alike, this shift has created a very different experience when opening packs. If you've ever felt like modern sets are harder to "complete" or that pull rates feel different than they used to, you're not imagining it.

In this article, we'll break down what has changed in modern Pokémon TCG sets and what it means for collectors.

## The Rise of the Chase Card

Older Pokémon sets often had one or two standout cards, but modern sets frequently include **multiple tiers of rarity**:

- Ultra Rare
- Secret Rare
- Illustration Rare
- Special Illustration Rare
- Hyper Rare (Gold Cards)

This layered rarity structure means that even if you pull a rare card, there may still be an even rarer version of the same Pokémon available in the set.

For collectors, this creates the thrill of the hunt — but it also makes completing a master set significantly harder.

## Why Sets Keep Getting Bigger

Many recent Pokémon sets contain **over 200 cards**, sometimes pushing closer to 300 when secret rares are included.

There are a few reasons for this:

### More Artwork Variety

The Pokémon Company has embraced alternate artwork in a big way. Illustration Rares and Special Illustration Rares allow artists to create full-scene artwork that tells a story rather than simply featuring the Pokémon on a background.

These cards have quickly become fan favourites.

### Appealing to Multiple Audiences

Modern sets are designed for:

- Competitive players
- Casual collectors
- Long-term investors
- Artwork collectors

Larger sets allow Pokémon to include something for each group.

## Pull Rates and Collector Expectations

One of the most common discussions among collectors is **pull rates**.

While official pull rates are never published, community data suggests that:

- Ultra Rares appear fairly regularly
- Illustration Rares are moderately rare
- Special Illustration Rares can be extremely difficult to pull
- Gold cards are among the rarest cards in a set

This means that opening booster boxes is less about completing a set and more about **chasing memorable pulls**.

For many collectors, the excitement now lies in the possibility of pulling a single standout card rather than filling a binder page.

## The Impact of Alternate Art Cards

Alternate art cards have arguably become the defining feature of modern Pokémon TCG sets.

Cards like these stand out because they:

- Feature detailed full-art illustrations
- Often tell a small story within the card
- Are printed in relatively low quantities

Some of these cards quickly become the **most valuable cards in their respective sets**, even surpassing traditional secret rares.

## Tips for Collectors Approaching New Sets

If you're planning to collect modern Pokémon sets, there are a few strategies that can help:

### 1. Decide Your Collecting Goal Early

Ask yourself what you're aiming for:

- Master set
- Favourite Pokémon
- Chase cards
- Artwork collection

Your goal will determine how you approach opening packs or buying singles.

### 2. Don't Be Afraid to Buy Singles

Because pull rates are unpredictable, many collectors now open a few packs for fun and then purchase the remaining cards they want individually.

This is often **far cheaper than chasing pulls**.

### 3. Watch Early Price Trends

The first few weeks after a set releases often see large price swings. Some cards spike early and settle later, while others slowly rise as collectors realize how rare they are.

Tracking these trends can help you decide when to buy.

## The Future of Pokémon TCG Sets

The Pokémon TCG continues to evolve with every new release. Illustration-heavy cards, premium rarities, and creative set design have made modern expansions some of the most exciting in the game's history.

For collectors, the key is understanding how these changes affect the hobby. Whether you're opening packs for the thrill or building a carefully curated collection, modern sets offer more variety than ever before.

And with new sets releasing multiple times a year, there's always another chase card just around the corner.
